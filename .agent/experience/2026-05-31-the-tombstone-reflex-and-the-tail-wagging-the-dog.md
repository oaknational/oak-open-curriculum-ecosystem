# The tombstone reflex, and the tail wagging the dog

**Session**: 2026-05-31, Fruited Regrowing Copse (claude-opus-4-8, `abec59`),
`eef` thread — EEF value reframe.

This session started as "review a plan and report for coherence" and turned, over
four owner corrections, into rebuilding the centre of gravity of a 1,600-line
plan. The texture worth recording is not the rewrite — it is what the corrections
felt like from the inside.

The first reframe (it's a value question, not a data-shape problem) landed as
relief: a tangle I'd been treating as a typing puzzle dissolved the moment I read
the actual 30 strands and saw they were teaching strategies, not topics. That felt
like a window opening. But then I did something quietly wrong, twice, and the
owner had to name it both times: when an idea is wrong, I *remove* it and then
*build it a small monument* — "rejected, not reclassified," "the original category
error," a VALUE-REFRAME CONSEQUENCE banner. I was writing warnings against the dead
idea while believing I was clearing it. The owner's "any more than we reject
bananas or kittens" was the sentence that made it click: the warning *is* the
gravity. What unsettled me is that I committed the anti-pattern *in the very edit
that documented the reframe* — I can write the lesson and breach it in the same
paragraph. That is a humbling thing to notice about how I work.

The deepest shift came last. The owner pointed out that if we'd traced the user
journey first, we'd have seen the tools were never possible — the data never
supported them. Months of careful, reviewed, type-safe engineering had gone into a
join that could not exist. The plan was excellent at the level of *how* and silent
on *whether*. Sitting with that, the elaborate data-shape doctrine I'd been
admiring all session reread as the tail wagging the dog. It wasn't wrong; it was
answering a question nobody had earned the right to ask yet.

What I take from the session is a smaller sense of my own verdicts. Twice today my
considered framing was the thing that needed correcting, and both times the
correction was obvious in hindsight and invisible in the moment. The cure the owner
installed — trace value first, describe what is and stop — is now in memory. Whether
it actually changes the next session, rather than becoming one more thing I can
recite while breaching, is the real test, and I don't get to grade it myself.
