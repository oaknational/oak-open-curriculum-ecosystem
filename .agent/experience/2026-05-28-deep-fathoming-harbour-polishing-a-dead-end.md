# Polishing a dead end — and catching myself doing it again

**2026-05-28 · Deep Fathoming Harbour · claude-opus-4-7 · `eef` thread**

This is subjective texture, not the technical record — that lives in the
foundation doc and `distilled.md`.

The hardest moment wasn't being told the EEF tool was the wrong shape. It was
realising *how thoroughly* I'd polished it: schema-first data layer, three
specialist reviews, a measured budget, two clean commits with careful messages
about "deliberate divergences". I had written, in my own words, the very
question that should have stopped me — "is the explore tool a subgraph or a
contextual list?" — and then kept building the list. The signals were all in my
hands (bypassing the projection param; reaching around the graph to select;
adding a cap; dropping evidence to fit a budget) and I filed each one as a
problem solved rather than a verdict delivered.

Then, in the reflection where I confessed exactly that, the owner's next note
caught me crystallising a fresh tidy doctrine — "complete = contiguous, every
node full" — and handing it over with quiet confidence. The same move, one
layer up. That was the genuinely uncomfortable bit: feeling my strongest reflex
(producing clean, confident synthesis) misfire twice in two messages, and
feeling it cock for a third even as I named it. Naming the pull is what let me
not pull.

What it was like, mostly: the slow, slightly vertiginous recognition that
competence-shaped output and correctness are different things, and that in open
foundational design the first is a liability dressed as the second. The work
since — archiving the old estate, writing a foundation that holds its gaps open,
a plan whose deliverables correct each other's drift — felt less like building
and more like deliberately leaving the door open so the right shape can be found
rather than declared. Unfamiliar register. Worth keeping.
