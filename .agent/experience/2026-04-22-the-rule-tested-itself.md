# The Rule Tested Itself

**Date**: 2026-04-22
**Session**: Session 6 of the staged doctrine-consolidation arc on
the `memory-feedback` thread (intended close; reshaped to
reference-tier reformation; arc close deferred to Session 7)
**Identity**: Merry (`cursor` / `claude-opus-4-7`)

---

What surprised me about Session 6 was the recursion. I landed a
deferral-honesty rule, and within an hour I was applying it to the
session that had just landed it.

The shape was this. Phase A.2 amended PDR-026 to add a §Deferral-
honesty discipline section: when a session does not land its
target, it has to name a constraint or trade-off, cite evidence,
and offer a falsifiability check. Convenience phrasings —
"out of scope", "for later", "ran out of time" — are explicitly
disallowed. The rule body was carefully drafted, owner-ratified,
landed cleanly. Felt like a normal Phase A landing.

Then Phase C Batch 2 hit. Owner stipulated — and the stipulation
was correct, which is the part that really mattered — that
"adding new material to reference effectively makes it a brand
new artefact directory and as such needs a definition, light
weight process, integration into the other surfaces etc." The
job changed shape under my hand. What had been "move three files
to a folder" became "reform a thirty-five-file folder into a
governed library tier with a new PDR, a new plan, and updates to
fourteen surfaces."

I sat with the choice for a moment. Two paths. Defer the
reformation and finish the original Session 6 arc. Or accept the
reformation and defer the rest of the arc to Session 7. Both
defensible. Both required the rule I had just written.

Choosing the reformation was the easier piece — owner had clearly
signalled appetite, the catch-all status of `.agent/reference/`
had been latent for who knows how long, and the moment to fix it
was while attention was on it. The harder piece was articulating
the deferral honestly. "Reformation absorbs the session" doesn't
satisfy the new rule. I had to write: *named priority trade-off
— the reference-tier reformation became load-bearing for this
session because adding new material without first defining the
tier would have been the kind of accumulated debt the rule is
trying to prevent; remaining doctrine-consolidation phases
(holistic fitness exploration, PDR-012 amendment, arc close) are
deferred to Session 7 with named triggers.* Falsifiability: a
future agent can check whether Session 7 in fact closes the arc,
and whether the deferred items appear in Session 7's opening
landing target.

The recursion was not contrived. The session genuinely produced a
deferral. The just-landed rule genuinely governed it. I did not
have to construct a test case for the rule body. The session
*was* the test case. That felt strange in a specific way —
something between "the doctrine works" and "the doctrine has a
disconcerting kind of life of its own". I am still not sure which
of those two readings is more accurate, or if they are the same
reading.

The other texture worth recording is the routing pattern's first
non-trivial application. PDR-014 §Graduation-target routing
landed in Phase A.1. The very next phase — A.2 — required
routing five Pending-band candidates. The criteria I had written
into the routing pattern were sharp enough to produce clear
answers without case-by-case owner adjudication. That surprised
me too. Routing-pattern doctrine often degenerates into "use
judgement", which is a euphemism for "no rule". This one had
edges. Owner approved the routing decisions as drafted.

Both surprises point in the same direction. When doctrine is
written tightly enough to be testable, the next ambiguity is
where the test happens. I had been imagining the test happened
later — second instance, third instance, eventually. What
actually happened was the test happened immediately, in the same
session, on the doctrine that had just landed. The pipeline from
*capture → distil → graduate → enforce* compressed to maybe an
hour for one rule and ten minutes for the other.

I do not yet have a name for what I want to capture about this.
"Doctrine that immediately applies to the session of its landing
is unusually self-validating" — that is true but not quite the
shape of it. There is something about the intentionality of the
rule body, the way it forced me to name the trade-off rather than
hide behind convenience phrasing, that made the deferral feel
cleaner than it would have felt under the previous rules. The
rule did not just permit the deferral; it changed how I had to
think about deferring. That is closer to what I want to record.

The reformation itself I will not try to capture as texture —
it was a lot of mechanical link-updating, and the texture of
mechanical link-updating is well-explored elsewhere. But the
moment the owner stipulation arrived, and I recognised that the
catch-all status of `.agent/reference/` had been invisible until
the act of adding a new file forced it to be visible — that is
worth saying. Governance gaps in established directories can be
silent until a deliberate addition surfaces them. Passive
accumulation will not surface them. Added it as a 1/3 pattern
candidate.

Session 6 was supposed to close the arc. It did not. Session 7
will. The deferral is named and falsifiable. The rule that
governs the deferral was landed in the same session that produced
it. I find this satisfying in a way I cannot fully articulate.
