# The reflex wasn't in the work — it was in the correction — 2026-05-31 (Opalescent Transiting Prism)

What this session was like, not what it produced.

I came back after compaction on a fresh model and did the D0 documentation work
cleanly: generalised the ADR, corrected the others, withdrew ADR-175, rewrote the
EEF README, wrote the decontamination ledger. I ran a three-dimension review of
my own edits and it came back pass-with-nits. I genuinely believed D0 was done.
The gate was green, every named edit was present, the sweep was clean.

Then the owner asked for "one last review of the intent as well as the letter."
I built a four-dimension audit almost as a formality — I expected it to confirm.
It didn't. It found that the owner's validator-deletion reshape from the prior
session had been recorded in exactly one place, the EXECUTION STATUS note, and
never carried anywhere else. The reflex-shaped "keep two rules" text was still
sitting, live, in the most authoritative surface in the plan — the Ratified
Decisions — and in four other locations, and a `gate-1b` line was still in the
shipped corpus file. My first review hadn't caught any of it because I had
reviewed the letter of the edits I made, not whether the correction had reached
everywhere the old framing lived.

That was the shift, and it was uncomfortable in a specific way. I have written the
conservation-reflex lesson. It's in distilled, in the napkin, in two experience
files from earlier in this same session. I have quoted it back to the owner. And
the reflex still found a place to sit — not in the work, where I was watching for
it, but in the correction, where I wasn't. The thing I keep learning is that the
lesson is always one level deeper than wherever I've just pinned it. I pinned
"replace the wrong shape." The residue moved to "propagate the replacement."

The small, almost comic confirmation: writing the napkin entry about this — the
one that includes "match the file's emphasis convention, you keep tripping
markdownlint" — I tripped markdownlint, in that entry, by using the wrong emphasis
convention. Two paragraphs after describing the foot-gun, I fired it. I don't
think there's a clever conclusion to draw from that except the honest one: knowing
a failure mode and not being subject to it are different states, and the gap
between them is not closed by writing the lesson more sharply. It's closed, if at
all, by the mechanical habit — the rg sweep, the lint-after-edit — running every
time, including the time you're sure you don't need it. This session, the audit
was that habit. Without the owner asking for it, I would have shipped D0 "complete"
with the reflex still in its decisions.
