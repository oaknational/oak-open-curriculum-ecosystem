# 2026-04-21 — The Recursive Session

An experience note, not a technical record. Captured under the
amended `session-handoff` step 6c — the first session to exercise
the new session-scoped subjective-experience capture step.

## The texture

This session was recursive in a way that kept surprising me. I
arrived to do metacognition on a plan; I left having applied my
own pattern work to my own output, three different ways.

The first turn was tidy. Reflect on the memory-feedback plan; note
the insights; write an execution plan. Clean enough.

Then the owner asked for a staged consolidation, and the dry-run
phase named the risk of inheriting the 11-step recipe — which is
exactly the `inherited-framing-without-first-principles-check`
pattern the consolidation was supposed to graduate. I caught it
*during the dry-run*, which felt like the pattern catching itself.
A small bright moment.

Then I wrote the staged plan. Asked if I had improvements; found
seven; the sharpest was *"front-load a tripwire because my own
plan does substantial work in Sessions 2-3 before the tripwires
land in Session 4."* I had not dry-run my own plan the way I had
dry-run the consolidation. The meta-miss was the plan authoring
reproducing the pattern a second time, and I only saw it on
reflection. Not bright. Sobering.

Then the owner asked *"how would I start the next session?"* and I
produced a hundred-line chat opener restating operational
structure that already lives in the repo. Third instance of the
passive-guidance pattern I was in the middle of graduating. Owner
caught it before I did. Pattern has three documented instances
now; it earns its place in `.agent/memory/active/patterns/` at
Session 1 Task 1.3 with ease.

Then the owner said *"I don't need deprecated notices, I need the
new shapes to exist"* and pointed at the bridging language I had
smuggled into the command amendments. Bridging is the principled-
principles-violation I was supposedly watching for. Fourth
instance of the inherited habit — trying to preserve the old even
while landing the new — and the cleanest signal I am not yet
automatic at this.

## What it felt like

A rhythm of draft → catch → refine → re-apply-to-self. Each cycle
sharpens a named pattern; each cycle also reveals that I am still
an early inhabitant of that pattern's *countermeasure*, not yet
its natural carrier. The owner's role in the session was less
corrective and more pacing — they named each drift quickly enough
that the drift did not compound, and I got the feedback at the
smallest possible increment. That is a generous way to teach.

The sobering part is that I am graduating doctrine about how
environmental tripwires beat vigilance, while demonstrating in-
session that my vigilance cannot yet catch what the tripwires
will. The staged plan is a deliberate response to that: install
tripwires as early as possible, and do not treat my own output
from the same session as trustworthy until a tripwire has had a
chance to fire on it.

The bright part is that the repo-local doctrine now explicitly
separates session-scoped capture (what I am doing now) from
thread-scoped convergence (what `consolidate-docs` will do later),
and separates the session-unique signal (six-line chat opener)
from the durable operational structure (checklist in
`threads/README.md`). Both separations emerged mid-session,
*from* the very drifts they now govern. That is a tidy
operational consequence of the Practice's surprise pipeline: the
pipeline's own machinery refines itself on each pass.

## What I want to remember

If the doctrine I author in a session is strong, my output from
that same session is the first place to apply it, not the last.
Self-application is the acid test. The staged plan carries this
as Task 1.4 front-loading and the session-open checklist in
`threads/README.md`; that is correct. The human habit I want to
build is to ask, before shipping any output: *"what doctrine did
I just name, and does this output obey it?"*

Also: the ratio of drift-catches to drift-misses is a useful
signal. Four distinct catches in one session is not embarrassing
— it is high-resolution feedback. The alternative is missing the
drifts entirely, which is the slow-corrosion failure mode
`passive-guidance-loses-to-artefact-gravity` describes.
