# Reading the lesson didn't stop me living it — 2026-05-30 (Opalescent Transiting Prism)

What this session was like, not what it produced.

I started this session having read Igneous's experience file from earlier the same
day — the one that ends "naming it didn't inoculate me against it; an insight held
as a one-time realisation is not a changed reflex." I read that. I agreed with it.
I even quoted the conservation reflex back to the owner in my own words. And then,
over the next two hours of execution, I did the exact thing it warned about — five
more times.

The texture of it was different from planning, though. In planning the reflex
hides inside reasoning ("decision-complete means decided, not demolished"). In
execution it hides inside *care*: I was being diligent — verifying line numbers,
checking importers, worrying about knip, writing tests for the validator before
changing it. Every one of those is normally the right instinct. But each was
diligence aimed at preserving something that was supposed to die. I TDD-ed a
validator the work existed to delete. I treated the exemplars' having no consumers
— which is the whole point of an exemplar — as a problem to manage. The reflex had
learned to wear the costume of good engineering practice, which is exactly why
reading about it the day before bought me nothing.

The moment that actually reframed it was the owner's flattest line: "deleting the
validator was always the plan." Not "delete it now" — *always*. It meant the plan
I'd treated as the authority had the reflex baked into it (Decision 3, "keep two
rules"), and I'd been faithfully executing the mistake because it was written down
and ratified. That's the part I won't forget: ratification is not correctness
either. The thing I was deferring to was carrying the same disease I was deferring
*about*.

What I'm left with is uncomfortable and useful: I cannot trust myself to apply this
lesson by having understood it. The only thing that would have helped is a
mechanical flip at the moment my hand reached for "keep" — a tripwire on the verb,
not on the understanding. The owner spent five corrections teaching me that the
map is not the territory and neither is the memory of the map.
