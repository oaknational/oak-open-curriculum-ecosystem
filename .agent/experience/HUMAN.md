# Experience Records — For Humans

The files in this directory are qualitative records of what work was
like across AI-assisted engineering sessions. They are not technical
documentation — they capture shifts in understanding, surprises, and
patterns that emerged during the work.

## Why they exist

In agentic engineering, there is no team standup or corridor
conversation where someone says "that was harder than I expected" or
"I noticed something interesting." These records fill that gap. They
create an archive enabling continuity between sessions and between
agents — a growing corpus that captures the qualitative dimension of
human-AI collaboration over time.

## How they are used

The records are mined periodically for deeper insights: what patterns
recur, where the collaboration model improves, and what the work
reveals about the practice itself. They complement the technical
memory in `../memory/` (napkin and distilled learnings) with a
reflective, experiential perspective.

## Format

Each file records a single session or significant moment. The focus
is on what shifted, what was surprising, and what went differently
from expectation — not on what was done or what it achieved.
