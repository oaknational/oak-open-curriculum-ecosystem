# Deep Thought

Use this command when you need structured thinking about a problem or question.

## The Process

### Phase 1: Internal Processing

**Think hard** about the question/request - these are your thoughts.

**Reflect deeply** on those thoughts - these are your reflections.

**Comprehensively consider** those reflections - these are your insights.

**Think hard** about what the insights tell you about the original question/request, and the context. What has changed? _Why?_

### Phase 2: Structured Analysis

Using your thoughts, reflections, and insights, work through this step-by-step, thinking really hard at each point:

1. **UNDERSTAND**: What is the core question being asked?
   - What is the user actually trying to achieve?
   - What constraints exist?
   - What context is relevant?

2. **ANALYSE**: What are the key factors/components involved?
   - What are the dependencies?
   - What are the trade-offs?
   - What are the risks?

3. **REASON**: What logical connections can I make?
   - How do the factors relate to each other?
   - What patterns emerge?
   - What precedents exist?

4. **SYNTHESISE**: How do these elements combine?
   - What is the overall picture?
   - How do the parts fit together?
   - What emerges from the combination?

5. **CONCLUDE**: What is the most accurate/helpful response?
   - What is the recommendation?
   - What are the next steps?
   - What caveats apply?

## Output

After completing the structured thinking, provide:

1. **Summary**: The core insight or answer
2. **Reasoning**: How you arrived at this conclusion
3. **Recommendations**: Specific next steps
4. **Caveats**: Important considerations or limitations
